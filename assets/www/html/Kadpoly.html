Background

The idea to start a Technical Institution in Northern Nigeria started as far back as 1951, which resulted into the establishment of Kaduna Technical Institute in 1956. The establishment of Technical Institute, Kaduna was as a result of the acceptance by the British Government on the recommendation of the Higher Education Commission, which suggested the upgrading of Yaba Higher College to Technical Institute and proposed Technical Institutes in Kaduna and Enugu. The Northern Nigeria Executive Council at its meeting of 17th August, 1962, Conclusion No.1, re-designated the Technical Institute, Kaduna as the Polytechnic, Kaduna. It became Kaduna Polytechnic in 1968 by the Federal Government Decree No. 20 of 1968, which was revised in 1979 by Decree .No. 79. In 1991, the institution was taken over by the Federal Government under Decree No. 40 of the same year. The institution was established with the objective of providing diverse instruction, training and research in technology, the sciences, commerce, the humanities and programmes of in-service instruction for members of the public service in Nigeria. In 1968, it amalgamated two training centres, namely; the College of Science and Technology and Staff Development Centre, with Survey Unit joining later. These formed the nucleus of its four- college structure then. Kaduna Polytechnic outside the Ahmadu Bello University (ABU) Zaria, is known to be the biggest higher institution in Nigeria.

The monumental growth and achievements, physically and intellectually have proved that the institution has maintained its Spider image. The “web” has spread from Nigeria to Africa and beyond.

Our Vision

Kaduna Polytechnic is envisioned to be a key player in the education and integral formation of professionally competent, service-oriented, principled, and productive citizens. Through its tri-fold function of teaching and training, research, and extension service, the institution aims to provide a system that is characterised by high public esteem and demand with high internal efficiency. It will be a prime mover of the nation’s socioeconomic growth and sustainable development through the provision of trained and competent manpower. The Polytechnic hopes, over the early years of this new century, to consolidate its leadership in flexible off-campus, on-campus and online learning.

Our Mission
  1. Prepare Nigerians regardless of gender, social-economic status, creed and ethnic affiliation, to take control of their own destiny, liberating them from dependency and endowing them with initiative, creativity, critical thinking, entrepreneurial skills, democratic values and an appreciation of work and the world of work
  2. To educate and train Nigerians for higher productivity and responsible citizenship in an environment where educational access is equitable;
  3. To accelerate the development of professionals ready to meet international competition;
  4. To inculcate nationalism and patriotism in the hearts and minds of the students and graduates
  5. To provide expanded access for lifelong learning and training.

Admissions

REQUIREMENTS

UNDERGRADUATE PROGRAMMES

Applicants must possess a minimum of five (5) credits at WASSCE/NECO/GCE/NABTEB/SISC Examinations or their equivalent in not more than two (2) sittings in the subjects relevant to their proposed Undergraduate Programmes.

English Language at credit level is compulsory for all candidates. In addition, credit in Mathematics is compulsory for candidates seeking admission into OND & HND Programmes in the Colleges of Science & Technology, Business & Management Studies and Engineering Courses.

Direct Entry admission is also available for applicants with suitable qualifications who obtained Joint Admissions and Matriculation Board (JAMB) DE Form 2018.

REMEDIAL PROGRAMMES

The Remedial programmes available in the 2017/2018 academic session are designed for candidates who fall into any of the following categories:

1. Candidates with relevant five (5) ‘O’ Level credit passes but scored below the minimum score in the 2015 JAMB (UTME).

2. Candidates with at least three (3) ‘O’ Level credit passes and as well as deficiency in JAMB (UTME).

3. Candidates with Ordinary National Diploma (ND) or National Certificate of Education (NCE) but deficient in ‘O’ Level credit passes requirement.

METHOD OF APPLICATION

1. You are expected to generate a reference code to accept your admission offer

2. Make your payment through any of the methods indicated.

3. Return back to the Portal and validate your payment and Print your Acceptance Slip.

4. Proceed to your department for clearance and obtain your login Code (Password)

5. Proceed to fill the Just Admitted Candidates Integration Module (JACI) to obtain your Matric Number so as to enter your portal

6. Wait for 24 hours then go to Academics Affairs Division to collect your Admission Letter.

7. In your portal after login, click on My Fees Menu and select the current session then click on Check Fees Button to generate your payment slip to pay your registration fees. Follow step 2 to Pay your fees.

8. Click on My Registration to carry out your courses registration

MODE OF PAYMENT

ALL PARENTS, GUARDIANS AND STUDENTS ARE HEREBY INFORMED THAT ALL PAYMENT OF SCHOOL FEES FOR THE 2017/2018 ACADEMIC SESSION SHOULD BE MADE BY BANK DRAFTS FROM ANY NIGERIAN BANK PAYABLE TO ‘KADUNA POLYTECHNIC, KADUNA’

SUCH DRAFTS SHOULD THEN BE PRESENTED TO THE BURSARY DEPARTMENT FOR RECEIPT AND INTRODUCTION TO ON-LINE REGISTRATION OF COURSES.

DEBTOR STUDENTS ARE ADVISED TO CLEAR THEIR ARREARS TO ENABLE THEM ENJOY THE FULL SERVICES AND BENEFITS AVAILABLE IN THE SCHOOL

PLEASE NOTE THAT DRAFTS SHOULD BE MADE SEPERATELY FOR EACH STUDENT WHERE A SPONSOR HAS MORE THAN ONE STUDENT IN THE SCHOOL.

PLEASE NOTE ALSO THAT THERE CAN BE NO COURSE REGISTRATION UNTIL PAYMENT OF SCHOOL FEES HAS BEEN EFFECTED.
